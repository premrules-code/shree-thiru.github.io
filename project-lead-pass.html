The Problem

Salesforce was sunsetting the Tableau Data Cloud SKU, leaving thousands of Tableau sales reps with no structured way to hand off interested Data Cloud customers to the right specialist. Data Cloud, one of Salesforce's fastest-growing products, was contributing only 5% of total pipeline, far below its potential.

Without a formal handoff mechanism, reps had no visibility or financial credit after passing a lead, which killed incentive to refer deals and silently destroyed pipeline that should have converted to revenue.

What Was Built

A fully automated, end-to-end lead referral platform inside Salesforce Org62, delivered in 4 weeks with a small, focused team:

  • A custom Lead Pass object in Org62 with a dedicated Lightning Record Page
  • A "Data Cloud Lead Pass" button on Contact records, visible only to eligible Tableau AEs via role-based access
  • An eligibility validation engine that checks user and account qualification before submission
  • A guided screenflow with qualifying questions for the Tableau AE
  • Real-time Slack notifications to Core AEs and Data Cloud AEs with accept/reject buttons
  • Auto-creation of a Stage 02 sales opportunity upon lead acceptance
  • A compensation reporting view for proper incentive tracking
  • FY24 historical data migration of all eligible open opportunities at go-live

Key Outcomes

  • Launched a fully automated lead referral platform targeting growth beyond the 5% pipeline baseline, positioning the business to close 10-20% of a ~$100M opportunity in FY25
  • Achieved 100% capture of all eligible FY24 open opportunities into the new system at go-live
  • Replaced an informal, invisible handoff process with a transparent, accountable workflow
  • Target: >50% adoption among eligible Tableau AEs in first 60 days, <2 business day median response time

Lessons Learned

Data hygiene was a hidden dependency: Auto-routing leads to the right specialist relied entirely on account team records being accurate. Clean data proved as critical as the product itself.

Backend validation rules nearly blocked launch: Automatic opportunity creation hit Salesforce validation rules that surfaced late, requiring a full audit of downstream dependencies.

Parallel enablement was key: Sales enablement and training ran alongside the build so reps were ready globally on day one.

Team

1 PM, 1-2 BT Engineers, 1 GSX Lead, 1 Enablement Lead, plus a separate data migration resource.
