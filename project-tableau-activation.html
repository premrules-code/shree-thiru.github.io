The Problem

Salesforce leadership needed a comprehensive 360-degree view of the user activation process for a massive migration: 40,000 sales reps and sales managers being moved in cohorts from a legacy CRM to a brand-new, AI-powered Salesforce Org.

Without this visibility, leadership was making cohort decisions on gut feel rather than evidence, and enablement teams were buried in manual spreadsheet-based reporting.

What Was Built

A cohort analytics application connecting enterprise data warehouse pipelines to a Tableau dashboard suite:

  • Overall Platform Activation Progress tracking showing activated users (11,240), in-progress (3,240), and remaining (25,520) out of 40,000
  • Activation breakdowns by Segment: AMER 37%, EMEA 29%, APAC 12%, LATAM 12%, JAPAN 4%
  • Breakdowns by Selling Role: Sales Cloud AE 40%, Service Cloud AE 26%, Marketing AE 15%, Tableau AE 5%, and more
  • A "Next Cohort Planner" ranking groups by ACV (low risk first) with Group, Segment, Role, Industry, ACV, Reps count, and Risk level
  • An ACV Analytics Dashboard with donut charts showing $22.4B total ACV across Product, Market Segment, Industry, and Selling Role
  • Interactive parameters enabling teams to slice data by segment, role, and ACV to identify variance

Key Outcomes

  • Eliminated manual reporting entirely, freeing 1,000+ hours annually for enablement teams
  • Replaced spreadsheet-driven guesswork with a live, executive-ready insights platform
  • Turned a high-risk deployment process into a data-driven, repeatable playbook for low-risk user activation at scale

Lessons Learned

Real-time refresh was unsustainable at scale: The team made a deliberate tradeoff to move to overnight refresh cycles, prioritizing data reliability and dashboard stability over immediacy.

GTM-driven dataset changes created fragility: A single schema change could silently break downstream insights. This was resolved by establishing a formalized change process and RACI.

The core tension: Moving fast enough to keep dashboards relevant without sacrificing the data integrity the entire migration strategy depended on.
