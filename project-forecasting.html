The Problem

Account sellers (product specialists) needed their own dedicated space to create a single shared forecast they could collaborate on with their leadership. The existing legacy system was a persistent source of forecast errors and manual rework.

The rapidly changing GTM org structure made maintaining alignment even harder. With 5,000+ product specialists globally, there was no unified way to blend data with expert insights for accurate revenue planning.

What Was Built

A Specialist Forecasting Platform built on Salesforce's Collaborative Forecasting capabilities:

  • A flexible, configuration-based solution to keep product specialist forecasts aligned with GTM changes
  • A complete data model including Forecasting Quota, Forecasting Item, Forecasting Adjustment, and Forecasting Owner Adjustment with parent/child hierarchies
  • The platform connects Sales, Operations, Finance, and Marketing in a continuous collaborative cycle
  • Structured RBAC for different personas (SEs, product leadership, product specialists)
  • Dashboard capabilities built on the specialist forecast data model for business health insights
  • Severity-based triaging routes to manage the high maintenance of the config tool

Key Outcomes

  • Adopted by 5,000+ product specialists globally
  • Achieved 99% data accuracy and 100% user adoption
  • Replaced a legacy system that had been a persistent source of forecast errors and manual rework
  • Delivered significant gains in planning efficiency by eliminating manual data reconciliation
  • Transformed how a global sales organization plans and predicts revenue

Lessons Learned

Config-based approach traded flexibility for maintenance: The deliberately chosen config approach handled a rapidly changing GTM org structure, but came with high maintenance overhead, managed by introducing severity-based triaging routes.

Scope expanded beyond original plan: Other stakeholders (SEs, product leadership) needed to collaborate through the tool, requiring structured RBAC for multiple personas.

One forecast, one truth: "One forecast gets all product specialists and their leadership on the same page. Fewer misunderstandings, smoother execution, faster decisions."
